About RiseMeUp

Committed to grow rural communities and invest in a brighter future for the Nuba Mountain people.

"Investing in rural people is investing in a brighter future"

Transforming Lives Through Sustainable Agriculture

RiseMeUp Sustainable Farming is a London-based non-profit charity dedicated to supporting the Nuba Mountain community in Sudan -- genocide survivors who deserve a chance to rebuild their lives with dignity and self-sufficiency.

We are transforming 130 acres of poor, non-productive land into a thriving agricultural hub through horticulture, farming, and green energy infrastructure, creating new opportunities for the neediest and most excluded members of this rural community.

Our Purpose

Vision & Mission

Where we are headed and how we plan to get there.

Our Vision

Taking steps towards sustainable development by developing 130 acres of poor-land into horticulture, farming, and implementing Green Energy as the main source of infrastructure for the farm -- creating a self-sustaining model of rural development.

Our Mission

Turning 130 acres of non-productive land into productive farmland, creating new agricultural job opportunities to help, support the neediest and excluded within this rural community -- working together to end poverty and build lasting food security.

What We Offer

Our Project Phases

A comprehensive four-phase plan to transform the Nuba Mountain community through sustainable agriculture.

Renewable Energy Hub
Phase 1

Biogas Plant

Our biogas plant uses all types of agricultural raw and residual materials to generate clean, renewable energy -- providing electricity, gas, and organic fertilizer for the farm.

Clean electricity generation
Natural gas production
Organic fertilizer output

The biogas facility is designed to process agricultural waste products, converting them into three valuable outputs: electricity for farm operations, gas for heating and cooking, and nutrient-rich fertilizer that returns to the soil. This closed-loop system dramatically reduces the community's dependency on external and costly resources, while promoting environmental sustainability and energy independence.

Modern Agriculture
Phase 2

10 Commercial Greenhouses

Modern, technologically efficient greenhouse structures equipped with the best equipment and advanced irrigation systems for intensive, year-round agriculture.

Off-season crop production
Climate and pest control
Higher quality produce

Our 10 commercial greenhouses represent a leap in agricultural capability for the region. With controlled environments, advanced irrigation, and modern growing technology, these structures enable year-round production of high-value crops. Benefits include off-season growing for market advantage, superior crop quality, precise climate control, integrated pest management, and efficient use of water and fertilizer resources.

Quality Assurance
Phase 3

Sorting & Packaging System

Large, fully automatic packaging lines designed to maintain consistent high-quality standards from farm to market, ensuring our produce reaches consumers in perfect condition.

Automated fabrication & cleaning
Filling, sealing & labeling
Overwrapping & palletizing

Our state-of-the-art sorting and packaging system handles the complete post-harvest process chain: fabrication, cleaning, filling, sealing, labeling, overwrapping, and palletizing. This fully automated operation ensures consistent product quality, extends shelf life, and enables the community to access wider markets with professionally packaged goods that meet international standards.

Food Security
Phase 4

Poultry Farm

A large-scale poultry operation providing essential protein and nutrition to the community, with both egg-producing and broiler hen facilities to ensure comprehensive food security.

80,000 egg-producing hens (2 halls)
50,000 broiler hens (2 halls)
Essential protein & nutrition

The poultry farm consists of two distinct operations: egg-producing hens housed in 2 halls with capacity for 40,000 birds each (80,000 total), and broiler hens in 2 halls with capacity for 25,000 birds each (50,000 total). Together, these facilities provide a reliable source of protein and nutrition for the community, contributing directly to food security and creating sustainable livelihoods for local farmers.
